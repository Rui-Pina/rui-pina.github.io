Hard book to review. On the Origin of Species often reads like Charles Darwin presenting possibilities rather than firm conclusions. He frequently admits uncertainty and notes the lack of definitive proof. His arguments rely largely on observation and personal reasoning, which sometimes makes the justification feel weak.

That said, the book undeniably laid a strong foundation for thinking about inheritance and genealogy (areas that were still poorly developed at the time). Rather than a doctrine everyone must accept, I see On the Origin of Species as a valuable framework for further inquiry into inheritance and the deeper question of where we come from, a line of thinking that has clearly influenced our modern studies of genealogy and species.
